About

Game-based learning is an area of educational science that has shown considerable promise in recent years. With lecturers and teachers facing the ever-present battle to engage students, game-based learning applications have emerged as a happy medium between fun and study. In theory, an engaging learning platform will directly result in greater student use, which will hopefully facilitate a greater acquisition of knowledge.

Lectic is a web application that provides a customisable game-based learning experience in the form of gamified quizzes. The games have been optimised for both mobile and desktop browsers, making them ideal for use by students in a lecture-setting or at home.

Quiz games can be added by lecturers, which can be given different levels. Students are initially only able play level 1 quiz games, but unlock higher levels through gaining coins. Coins are obtained though the completion of quiz games and awarded meritocratically based on the performance of a student. Through the use of unlockable levels, students will want to play a quiz over and over again, in order gain more coins and unlock higher levels.

In addition, a lecturer user will be able to view a leader-board of student performances in any given quiz. This allows a lecturer to assess the performance of their students, aiding them in identifying students who may be struggling.
